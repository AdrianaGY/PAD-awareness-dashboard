# Peripheral Artery Disease (PAD) Awareness Dashboard

This project presents an interactive dashboard examining awareness of Peripheral Artery Disease (PAD) among Hispanic/Latino adults in the United States using data from the Hispanic Community Health Study / Study of Latinos (HCHS/SOL).

The dashboard explores variation in PAD awareness by symptom status, country of origin, and clinical risk factors using survey‑weighted analyses.

## Data Source
Data come from the Hispanic Community Health Study / Study of Latinos (HCHS‑SOL), a population‑based cohort study of Hispanic/Latino adults conducted between 2008–2011 across multiple U.S. communities. Peripheral Artery Disease awareness was self‑reported. The dataset is publicly available for research purposes.

## Why This Matters
Low awareness of Peripheral Artery Disease limits early diagnosis and increases the risk of preventable cardiovascular complications. Identifying disparities in awareness can inform targeted public health interventions and improve cardiovascular outcomes in high‑risk populations.
